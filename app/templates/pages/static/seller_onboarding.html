    Opening a shop on SGT Cart takes about 30 minutes for form-filling, plus 1-2 business days for admin verification. This guide takes you from zero to live store and your first sale.

    Read the Seller Agreement and Listing Guidelines before you start — they define the rules you accept by listing.

    1. Who can sell on SGT Cart

    You qualify if you are…Required
    An individual sole trader resident in BangladeshAged 18+, valid NID, Trade Licence, TIN.
    A registered company / partnership / proprietorship in BangladeshRJSC certificate, Trade Licence, TIN, BIN (VAT) if applicable, authorised representative's NID.
    A non-resident Bangladeshi (NRB) with a registered local entitySame as company, plus operating address inside Bangladesh.

    Foreign-resident sellers will be onboarded under a separate cross-border addendum in a later phase. Until then, every Seller must have a verifiable Bangladesh business identity.

    2. Documents you'll need

    Gather these before you start — having them ready cuts the onboarding time in half:

    • NID — front + back, clear scan or photo (JPG/PNG, < 5 MB each).
    • Trade Licence — clear scan (PDF/JPG).
    • TIN certificate — issued by the National Board of Revenue.
    • BIN / VAT registration — if your annual turnover is above the NBR threshold (currently Tk 80 lakh for VAT-eligible categories).
    • Bank statement first page OR bKash Personal Retail / Nagad Merchant account screenshot — proves payout-account ownership.
    • Shop logo (PNG, transparent background, ≥ 256×256 px).
    • Shop banner (JPG, 1920×600 px recommended).
    • Storefront photo if you have a physical shop — helps customer trust.

    3. Step 1 — Create your account

    1. Visit /signup/?role=seller.
    2. Enter your business email, mobile number, and choose a password.
    3. Verify the email with the one-time code sent to your inbox.
    4. You're logged into the Seller Center at /seller/ in "Pending Verification" state. You can start uploading documents and drafting Listings while you wait.

    4. Step 2 — Submit KYC for verification

    1. From /seller/, tap Shop Verification (KYC).
    2. Choose your business type (individual / company / partnership).
    3. Upload your NID front + back; enter your NID number exactly as printed.
    4. Upload Trade Licence; enter the licence number.
    5. Upload TIN certificate; enter the TIN.
    6. Enter your full shop address (line, area, city, district, postal code).
    7. Submit.

    An SGT Cart admin reviews your submission within 1-2 business days. You'll receive an email when the decision is made. Common reasons for rejection: blurry document scans, NID name does not match Trade Licence name, expired Trade Licence. Fix and resubmit any time.

    5. Step 3 — Build your store profile

    While KYC is under review, set up the storefront — it goes live the moment KYC is approved.

    • Shop name (English + Bangla) — bilingual is strongly recommended; storefront ranks better in mixed-language search.
    • Shop slug — used in the URL /store/your-slug/. Choose a short, memorable form. Lowercase letters and hyphens only.
    • Description — a 2-3 paragraph introduction. What you sell, since when, where, what makes you different.
    • Logo — square, transparent PNG looks best.
    • Banner — wide cover image for your store page.
    • Phone & address — visible to admin only; never displayed to customers.

    Sneak preview your storefront at /store/your-slug/. Anyone with the link can see it once KYC is approved.

    6. Step 4 — List your first products

    1. From the Seller Center, tap My Products → Create.
    2. Fill in the title (EN + BN), category, base price, stock, SKU, description.
    3. Upload 1-8 product images. The first one becomes the cover.
    4. Add variants if applicable — colour, size, each with its own price and stock.
    5. Add specifications (key/value rows like "Material — Cotton 100%"; up to 20 rows).
    6. Set bulk-pricing tiers if you offer volume discounts.
    7. Tap Submit for review. An admin reviews — typically same day — and the product goes live.

    Read the Product Listing Guidelines before submitting. Common rejection reasons include: copied descriptions, watermarks on images, contact info in images or text, prohibited items, missing variants/specs that the category requires.

    Tip: List at least 5 products before you market your store. A store with one product looks abandoned and discourages customer trust.

    7. Step 5 — Set up payouts & tax

    1. From the Seller Center, open Payout Settings.
    2. Choose your primary payout method:
      • Bank account — name, account number, branch.
      • bKash — personal or agent number.
      • Nagad — merchant or personal number.
    3. Confirm your TIN; this is used by SGT Cart to deposit VAT withholding to NBR (where applicable).
    4. Read and accept the Seller Agreement.

    Earnings begin flowing the moment your first Sub-Order is delivered. See the Fees & Commission page for the commission breakdown and the Payouts page (coming soon) for the weekly settlement cycle.
